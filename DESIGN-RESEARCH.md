# Design research: Outsourced Employment

Date: 2026-07-21

## Search intent

Primary query: `outsourced employment services employer of record`

The homepage needs to help a business owner make sense of an overloaded category. "Outsourced employment" can mean an employer of record, contractor hiring, offshore staffing, or a managed service. The useful design job is not to make another software dashboard. It is to help the buyer name the model, define the role, and ask better questions before speaking with a provider.

## Five direct organic competitors inspected

### 1. Deel

URL: https://www.deel.com/hiring/

- Large sans serif headline, pale blue field, black pill actions, and country cards.
- Search and region filters make global coverage tangible.
- The page is clean but sparse. Tall cards carry little information, and the cookie panel obscured the first row during inspection.

### 2. Remote

URL: https://remote.com/global-hr/employer-of-record

- Deep burgundy hero with a global network illustration, worker portraits, and one repeated demo action.
- Strong visual ownership of the category through color and custom illustration.
- The globe composition communicates international hiring well, but its small labels are hard to read. Several media areas appeared blank or unloaded in the inspected capture.

### 3. Oyster

URL: https://www.oysterhr.com/product/employer-of-record

- Warm sand hero, charcoal type, acid-yellow accents, rounded product panels, and a cutout human portrait.
- Good balance of friendly editorial design and product detail. Human imagery keeps the employment topic from feeling like payroll software.
- The right side is busy, and the award badges add clutter, but the overall hierarchy is the strongest of the set.

### 4. Pebl (formerly Velocity Global)

URL: https://hellopebl.com/employer-of-record/

- Oversized editorial serif, mint action color, aqua gradient, and large rounded lifestyle photography.
- The type and color feel more approachable than most EOR sites.
- Two navigation rows, inconsistent action labels, and a generic office photo weaken the otherwise distinctive hero. The cookie panel blocked part of the value proposition during inspection.

### 5. Rippling

URL: https://www.rippling.com/products/payroll/employer-of-record

- Deep plum hero, orange actions, large product UI, and a dense customer-logo strip.
- Product mockups, review proof, and consistent modular cards make the page feel mature.
- The header is busy, the email-first form asks for commitment too early, and much of the interface text is too small to inspect.

## Chosen direction

Winner: **Oyster**

Oyster has the best fit for this site's job. Its warm palette and human-centered imagery make a technical employment decision feel understandable. The design also leaves room for product or process detail without turning the page into a wall of corporate software screenshots.

## Original design direction

The new homepage is an independent "employment field guide," not an EOR product clone:

- ink-blue editorial type on warm parchment;
- a cobalt action color and sharp coral details instead of Oyster's charcoal and acid yellow;
- a full-height, locally hosted image of a real remote team conversation;
- a layered "role brief" card over the image, replacing product dashboard mockups;
- a model selector laid out like annotated file tabs;
- an honest three-step path from role definition to provider questions;
- no pricing cards, customer logos, awards, review counts, savings claims, or invented performance figures.

The composition borrows the winner's warmth, rounded image treatment, and human/product balance. It does not copy Oyster's wording, brand assets, badge wall, map graphics, or exact layout.

## Copy standard

Touched homepage copy was edited for plain language. It avoids inflated claims, vague proof, forced three-part slogans, generic conclusions, and chatbot phrasing. Claims are limited to what the site can actually do: explain the models, help scope a role, and route a staffing-plan request.
